Privacy Policy

Version 2.0 · Effective April 17, 2026

1. What This Covers

This Privacy Policy explains how Groundlayer LLC ("Groundlayer," "we," "us") collects, uses, and shares information in connection with our web design, hosting, and related services. It applies to our business interactions with clients and prospects. It does not govern websites we build on behalf of clients (see Section 7).

2. Information We Collect

From clients and prospects directly:

  • Name, business name, email, phone number
  • Business address and website content you provide (text, photos, logos)
  • Billing details (Stripe customer token linked to your payment method; we do not store full card numbers)
  • Communications with us (email, text, chat)

Automatically, when you visit groundlayer.co:

  • Standard server logs (IP address, user agent, timestamps) from Cloudflare and GitHub Pages
  • Basic analytics (page views, referrer) using privacy-respecting tooling

Not collected: We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13 and do not set advertising or cross-site tracking cookies on groundlayer.co.

3. How We Use Information

  • Build, maintain, and update client websites
  • Process payments and manage billing
  • Communicate about projects, updates, and support
  • Improve our services and operations
  • Comply with legal, tax, and accounting obligations

We do not sell or rent personal information, share it with third parties for their marketing, or access client analytics or private data without authorization.

4. Legal Basis (EU/UK Visitors)

  • Contract - to perform services you engaged us for
  • Legitimate interests - routine operations, security, improvements
  • Consent - where required (non-essential cookies, marketing email)
  • Legal obligation - tax and accounting records

6. Data Retention

  • Active engagements: project files are retained for the duration of the engagement.
  • After termination: client files remain available for export for 90 days, then deleted from active systems. Routine backups may persist longer and are overwritten on normal cycles.
  • Financial and tax records: retained for at least 7 years as required by federal and state tax law. This overrides the 90-day rule for those specific records.
  • Communications: retained for 2 years after project completion unless longer retention is required by law.

7. Websites We Build for Clients

When we build a website for a client:

  • The client is the data controller for visitors to that website.
  • Groundlayer acts as a data processor, hosting and maintaining the site on the client's behalf.
  • If a client site collects personal information (contact forms, e-commerce, signups), the client is responsible for its own privacy policy, cookie banner, and handling of data-subject requests.
  • We will help clients add appropriate privacy notices; compliance remains the client's obligation.

8. Security

  • Websites we host are served over HTTPS/TLS.
  • Access credentials are stored in a password manager and protected with multi-factor authentication where supported.
  • Client GitHub repositories are private by default.
  • No system is completely secure. If we become aware of a breach affecting client personal data, we will notify affected clients without undue delay and notify authorities where legally required.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Request a copy of personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion (subject to legal retention requirements such as tax records)
  • Object to or restrict certain processing
  • Withdraw consent for processing based on consent
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (EU/UK) or your state attorney general (US)

To exercise any of these rights, email hello@groundlayer.co. We will respond within 30 days.

10. California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents have the right to know what personal information we collect, to request deletion, and to opt out of "sales" or "sharing" of personal information. We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined under California law. To make a request, email hello@groundlayer.co.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as our services evolve. We will notify clients of material changes by email. The effective date at the top reflects the most recent update. Prior versions are available on request.
