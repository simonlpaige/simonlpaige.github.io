About

Hi, I'm Simon.

S.L.P.

I was 14 when I figured out I could write programs on the TI-83 Plus I was supposed to be doing math on. Choose-your-own-adventure games first, then crude cartoon jokes drawn with the graph functions. Nobody told me what to make, so I made what I thought was funny.

A year later I was drawing comic strips in MS Paint, frustrated I couldn't get the panels to sit right on a page. That frustration was my introduction to layout. The fix was HTML. GeoCities page by 16.

Through my twenties I did freelance web work in Drupal, CSS, and WordPress. Hosted media libraries from servers in my own house, ran them through DynamicDNS, learned everything about the internet you can only learn by breaking it at 2 a.m.

Portland, 2002 to 2013. Bartended in the southeast, studied digital audio production and small business management, did indoor gardening automation on the side. Then back to KC.

A couple years of landscape design for Beaver Creative Environments, high-end residential. Same shop did custom furniture, so I milled wood for dining tables and conference tables for clients who could pick anything and picked custom. Loved the work. My back said no.

The last few years: control systems. ETC and Strand lighting, QSC audio, AMX and Extron, Dante and AVoIP on layer 2. Physical installs and sales with cities, counties, and governments. Taught me more about project management than any book did.

Websites were always what I came back to. Hand-coded, plain HTML, files on a server. Same satisfaction as a well-milled joint: the thing is made of what it looks like, and it'll last.

That's what I build for Kansas City small businesses now. Durable sites that won't break when some platform changes its mind. Alongside that I build Commonweave and Neighborhood OS, because the software neighborhoods actually need doesn't exist yet, and I'm tired of waiting for someone else to make it.

I'm most interested in building the tools that make the world a better place.
