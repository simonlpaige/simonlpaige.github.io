Sometimes I'm not your guy.

I get a few calls a month from people I'm not right for. Rather than waste your time on a discovery call, here's the honest list. If anything below matches, I've probably already told you what to do next.

Web design is one of those things where the answer really does depend on who you are. The same site that's great for a two-person landscaping crew would be the wrong tool for a twelve-person law firm, and the same tool that's wrong for the landscaper is probably right for someone else. I'd rather just tell you which is which than book a call and try to talk you into me.

You probably want Squarespace or Wix

If any of these sound like you.

You want to edit weekly

If you'll be changing text, photos, or prices yourself every few days, you want a drag-and-drop builder. That's what those tools are for.

You're testing an idea

If there's a real chance you'll kill this business in three months, don't spend build money. Spin up a free Squarespace and see if it takes.

Budget is under $300 all-in

My build floor is $399. If that's genuinely out of reach, Squarespace's $16/mo plan will get you a working site this weekend.

You need a blog with 50+ posts

Hand-coded isn't the right tool for a big content operation. Ghost, WordPress, or Squarespace are all better for that.

Nothing wrong with Squarespace for those cases. It's the right tool. The only reason I push back on it is when someone wants a durable, low-maintenance site and has been told a subscription builder is their only option. It isn't.

You want a bigger agency

If you need a team, not one person.

Complex e-commerce

Inventory syncing, multi-warehouse, returns, subscriptions, B2B pricing tiers. I do Stripe checkout for under 50 products. Anything beyond that, you want Shopify and a real Shopify agency.

Integrated marketing stack

CRM, email marketing, paid ads, attribution, all under one roof with a dashboard. That's a full marketing agency. I'm a website builder, not a CMO.

More than 20 employees

At that size you need account management, project managers, regular stakeholder reviews, the whole structure. I'm one person answering my own phone.

Budget is $10K+

If you have that much to spend, you should get more than one person's time for it. A real agency earns that money. I'd just be pocketing the margin.

For Kansas City agencies I respect enough to send work to, call me and I'll make an introduction. I won't list names on a public page without asking them first, but if you're the right fit for one of them, I know who to point you at.

You want a specialist

If you need someone who only does one thing.

A full Shopify store

I'll point you at a Shopify specialist. They live inside that ecosystem all day, and they're worth what they charge.

WordPress with complex plugins

Memberships, LMS, forums, booking systems with weird edge cases. WordPress world. I don't work in it.

A native mobile app

If your customer genuinely needs a phone app, not a website, you want a mobile developer. Different skill set, different price range.

Custom software or SaaS

If you're building a product, not a website, you want a software developer. Happy to chat about it, but I won't pretend I'm the right builder.

I am your guy if

Any of this sounds right.

You want one person who picks up

No account manager, no ticket system, no "let me loop in my team." You call, I answer, we figure it out.

You want a site that lasts

Plain HTML on a server works exactly as well in five years as it does today. No platform update is going to break it.

You want to own your stuff

You keep the code. You keep the domain. If we stop working together you walk away with everything.

Your business is local

If your customers live within 30 miles of your front door, I already get the shape of your problem. That's most of what I do.
